FAQs

Frequently Asked
Questions

Find answers to common questions about robotic surgery, consultations, and our services.

Robotic surgery is a minimally invasive technique where the surgeon controls robotic arms equipped with specialised instruments. Unlike traditional open surgery, robotic surgery uses tiny incisions (usually 1-2 cm), resulting in less pain, minimal scarring, reduced blood loss, shorter hospital stays, and significantly faster recovery. The robotic system provides 3D high-definition vision and enhanced precision beyond what the human hand can achieve alone.

Yes, robotic surgery is extremely safe when performed by a trained and experienced surgeon. Dr. Nemade has performed over 5,000 successful procedures with a 98% success rate. The robotic system offers enhanced precision, reduced risk of complications, and the surgeon maintains full control throughout the procedure.

Robotic surgery can address a wide range of conditions including hernia repairs, gallbladder removal, colorectal procedures, bariatric surgery, and various abdominal and pelvic conditions. During your consultation, Dr. Nemade will assess your specific condition and recommend the best approach.

Recovery time varies but is typically much shorter than traditional surgery. Many patients can return home within 1-2 days and resume normal activities within 1-2 weeks. Dr. Nemade provides personalised recovery plans and follow-up care to ensure optimal healing.
